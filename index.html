History

The Snack Wrap was a menu item that was available at McDonald's in the United States in 2006. It consists of 100% white meat chicken breast (crispy or grilled), lettuce, shredded cheddar jack cheese, Monterey Jack cheese, and sauce (ranch, honey mustard, or salsa roja), wrapped in a soft flour tortilla.

This site is a homage to the Snack Wrap. The Snack Wrap was arguable the best item that McDonald's has ever released and is missed and adored by millions of people. If you agree, sign the petition at the bottom of the page

Why Was It Discontinued?

The snack wrap was officially discontinued in 2016 because of underwhelming sales and its time consuming construction. Compared to other items, it took workers a significant time to construct. Workers would have to steam the tortilla, prepare the chicken, and then assemble and fold the wrap into the cardboard box.

McDonald's corporate policy allows for a maximum of 90 seconds for each customer at the drive through. But with the introduction of the snack wrap, this policy was nearly impossible to fulfill when a customer ordered a snack wrap. According to one franchisee owner, the snack wrap took at least three minutes to make.

According to a general manager in the Hastings Mcdonald's location, "It took 20 seconds alone to steam a McWrap tortilla. Chicken had to be chopped and stuffed inside, along with sauce, bacon, lettuce, tomato, and cucumbers, depending on which of the nine varieties of McWrap a customer ordered. The whole thing then had to be rolled tight enough to fit into a slim cardboard box designed to make McWraps easier to eat while driving. Tortilla rolling turned out to be an inexact science. Sometimes the finished wraps were too big for the boxes and had to be rerolled. Fitting them in quickly and consistently 'was a nightmare,'" (Annette Snyder, https://www.bloomberg.com/features/2015-mcdonalds-franchises).

Bring Back the Snack Wrap

NUTRITION

Americans are becoming more health conscious than ever. Many people are straying away from fast food because it is often deemed unhealthy. However, the snack wrap was relatively healthy when compared with many other items. A grilled chicken wrap was only 181 calories, 2.1 grams of fat, 0.79 grams of salt, but had 13 grams of protein. Bringing back this item would introduce a healthy menu option for McDonald's customers.

PRICE

Not only was the Snack Wrap relatively healthy, it was affordable as well. Back when it was sold in 2016, its price was $1.59. Adjusting for inflation, that comes up to around $2.09. Even rounding to $2.50, this item was very affordable and competitively priced.
